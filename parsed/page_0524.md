## 891 Variance of Sum of BM

## Prompt:

Let W t be a standard Brownian Motion. Compute Var ( W 1 + W 2 ) .

## Explanation:

The trick here is to note that W 1 + W 2 = ( W 2 -W 1 ) + 2 W 1 . We do this because of the fact that W 2 -W 1 and 2 W 1 are independent random variables. Therefore,

$$V a r ( W _ { 1 } + W _ { 2 } ) = V a r ( ( W _ { 2 } - W _ { 1 } ) + 2 W _ { 1 } ) = V a r ( W _ { 2 } - W _ { 1 } ) + V a r ( 2 W _ { 1 } ) = V a r ( W _ { 2 } - W _ { 1 } ) + 4 V a r ( W _ { 1 } )$$

We know that W 2 -W 1 ∼ N (0 , 1) , so the first term is 1 . We also know that W 1 ∼ N (0 , 1) so the second term is 4 · 1 = 1 . Adding these up, our total is 5 .

## 892 Defining Correlation

Company : None

## Prompt:

The correlation between X and Y is 0.56. What is the correlation between 8 X and Y +7 ?

## Explanation:

The correlation between X and Y is defined as the ratio of their covariance to the product of their standard deviations, none of which are affected by multiplication or addition of constants. The correlation is thus still 0 . 56 .
