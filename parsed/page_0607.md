## Prompt:

We say that a transition matrix P is doubly stochastic if 1) every entry is non-negative and 2) every row and column sums to 1 . Suppose that you have a Markov Chain with a doubly stochastic transition matrix P on S = { 1 , 2 , . . . , 100 } . Let π be the stationary distribution of this Markov Chain. Find || π || .

## Explanation:

The stationary distribution is a row vector satisfying π = π P . Writing this out in matrix multiplication form, we have that π j = 100 ∑ i =1 π i P ij . As we know 100 ∑ i =1 P ij = 1 from the fact that columns sum to 1 in a doubly stochastic matrix, suppose that π i = 1 for all i . Then

$$1 = \sum _ { i = 1 } ^ { 1 0 0 } P _ { i j } = 1$$

However, we note that 100 ∑ i =1 π i = 1 by definition of being a distribution. Therefore, the above allows us to conclude that π is uniform on S , so that π i = 1 100 for all i . This means that || π || = √ 100 · 1 100 2 = 1 10
