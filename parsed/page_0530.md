## Prompt:

You have access to a stock with price S 0 = 14 , a put at strike K = 17 with initial price P 0 = 5 . 9 , a put at strike K = 14 with initial price Q 0 = 2 . 1 , and bonds that pay out 1 at time T with initial value B 0 = 0 . 9 . Find the arbitrage.

Give the answer in the format of # Stock + # Put (K = 17) + # Put (K = 14) + # Bonds

## Explanation:

We have the following relationship that must hold for puts on the same underlying with different strikes.

$$P ( K _ { 2 } ) - P ( K _ { 1 } ) \leq ( K _ { 2 } - K _ { 1 } ) Z$$

If we let K 2 = 17 and K 1 = 14 and plug the values in. We get:

$$5 . 9 - 2 . 1 \leq ( 1 7 - 1 4 ) * 0 . 9 \\ 3 . 8 \leq 3 * 0 . 9 \\$$

This clearly does not hold. We should long the undervalued item and short the overvalued item. Here, we will long 3 units of the bond, short the K = 17 put and long the K = 13 put. This gives us:

$$\# \text { Stock } + \# \text { Put } ( K = 1 7 ) + \# \text { Put } ( K = 1 4 ) + \# \text { Bonds } = 0 - 1 + 1 + 3 = 3$$

## Prompt:

Two values X and Y are chosen uniformly at random between 0 and 1. What is the probability that the ratio X Y is in between 1 and 2?

## Explanation:

The ratio satisfies the constraint if and only if Y &lt; X and Y &gt; 1 2 X . The area covered by this constraint in the sample space X,Y ∈ [0 , 1] is equal to 1 2 -1 4 = 1 4 . Since both X and Y are uniformly distributed, the probability is 1 4 .
