## Prompt:

A dartboard consists of 3 concentric circular regions of radii 1 , 2 , and 3 . If you shoot 3 darts that land in a uniformly random spot on the dartboard, find the probability that each lands in a distinct region.

## Explanation:

The total area of the dartboard is 9 π , as the circles are concentric. The area of the circle of radius 1 is just π . The area of the second region is (2 2 -1 2 ) π = 3 π , as the circles are concentric. Lastly, the area of the third region is (3 2 -2 2 ) π = 5 π . Therefore, the probability of the dart landing in each of the three regions is, respectively, 1 9 , 3 9 , and 5 9 . There are 3! = 6 orders in which we can land in the three regions. Additionally, the probability of any such particular ordering is 1 · 3 · 5 9 3 = 5 243 . Therefore, the probability of landing in the three distinct regions in 3 shots is

$$3 ! \cdot \frac { 5 } { 2 4 3 } = \frac { 1 0 } { 8 1 }$$

## Prompt:

Let's say you have product A in which you are quoting 4 @ 5 and product B in which you are quoting 10 @ 12 . X @ Y means that X is our bid and Y is our ask. We want to make a market on the product A -B . What is the bid-ask spread you will quote? Give the answer in the format of Y 2 -X 2

## Explanation:

We want to quote a market on the product A -B . A naive answer may be to set our bid at 4 -10 = -6 and our ask at 5 -12 = -7 . However, this is incorrect. To replicate A -B , we need to long 1 unit of A and short 1 unit of B . Since we are market-makers, we will quote the bid of A -B to be Bid A -Ask B = 4 -12 = -8 since we are willing to buy A at the bid of A and sell B at the ask of B . Using similar logic, we can quote our ask to be Ask A -Bid B as we are now selling A and going long B . This gives us an ask of 5 -10 = -5 and a market of -8 @ -5 .

Plugging in our ask and bid, we get Y 2 -X 2 = ( -5) 2 -(8) 2 = -39 .
