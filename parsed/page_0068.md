## Prompt:

Consider the set Ω = { 1 , 2 , . . . , 20 } . Two subsets A and B of Ω are uniformly at random selected from the power set of Ω independently. It is possible that A = B . Define N = | A ∩ B | . Compute Var ( N ) E [ N ] .

## Explanation:

For an element x ∈ A ∩ B , we know that x ∈ A and x ∈ B . Since A and B are independently selected, the events { x ∈ A } and { x ∈ B } are independent as well. As the subsets are selected uniformly at random, each element has probability 1 2 of being in the subset or not being in the subset. Therefore, each of the 20 elements is in A ∩ B with probability 1 4 , independent between elements.

Therefore, | A ∩ B | just counts the number of successes (which are inclusions into the intersection) in 20 independent trials with probability 1 4 , so N ∼ Binom ( 20 , 1 4 ) . For X ∼ Binom ( n, p ) , E [ X ] = np and Var ( X ) = np (1 -p ) , so the ratio in question here is just 1 -p . As we know the value of p , our answer is just 1 -1 4 = 3 4 .
