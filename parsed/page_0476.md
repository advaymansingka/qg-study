## Prompt:

Which of the digits from 0 -9 (inclusive of both) does 2 29 not have? You may use the fact that 2 29 has 9 digits.

## Explanation:

Let S ( n ) be the sum of the digits of an integer n . The difference between any integer n and S ( n ) is divisible by 9 i.e. 9 | n -S ( n ) for any integer n . Let x be the missing digit. Then we know that as there are 9 digits and one is missing, the sum of all the digits in 2 29 is 45 -x . Plugging this in, 9 | 2 29 -(45 -x ) .

Now, note that 2 29 = 2 5 · (2 6 ) 4 = 2 5 · 64 4 = 2 5 · (63 + 1) 4 by basic rearrangement. By the Binomial Theorem, every single term in (63 + 1) 4 is divisible by 63 except the last term, which is 1 . Therefore, we can write (63 + 1) 4 = 63 k +1 for some integer k . Substituting this back in, we get that

$$2 ^ { 2 9 } = 2 ^ { 5 } \cdot ( 6 3 k + 1 ) = 6 3 \cdot 2 ^ { 5 } \cdot k + 2 ^ { 5 }$$

Subtracting 2 5 , we have that 2 29 -2 5 = 63 · 2 5 · k . This means that 9 | 2 29 -2 5 .

Using this fact, as we know 9 | 2 29 -(45 -x ) and 9 | 2 29 -2 5 , we know that 9 | 2 5 -(45 -x ) , as we just subtracted two values divisible by 9 . This means that 9 | x -13 . As x is a digit, it must be between 0 and 9 , inclusive of both. The only value where the above holds true in that range is x = 4 , so 4 is the missing digit.
