## 55 Matching Die Trio

Company : None

## Prompt:

Three fair 6 -sided dice are rolled and their upfaces are recorded. Find the probability that the values showing upon rolling all three dice again is the same as the original three values recorded.

## Explanation:

With three dice being rolled originally, we can either have all show the same value, have two distinct values, or have all three be distinct values. We condition on the the number of distinct values showing after the first roll.

The probability all of the values are the same is 6 6 3 = 1 36 , as you can roll the first die and the other two must match that value, which occurs with probability 1 6 per die. The probability that all three values are distinct is easily calculated to be 6 · 5 · 4 6 3 = 5 9 . Therefore, the probability that exactly two distinct values appear is 1 -1 36 -5 9 = 5 12 . Now, we must calculate the probability in each case that the second values match the first values.
