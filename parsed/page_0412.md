Now, let's deal with these sums individually. E [ X i Y i ] takes the value of the probability that both X i and Y i occur. For both X i and Y i to occur, one would need to roll BOTH a one and a five on a single turn i . There is no chance of being able to do this, so E [ X i Y i ] = 0 . Thus, the entire second sum evaluates to 0 . For the first expectation, it takes the value of the probability of both X i and Y j occurring. For both to occur, one would need to roll a 1 on roll i and a 5 on roll j .
