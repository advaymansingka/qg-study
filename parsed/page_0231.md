## Explanation:

We want to find E [( N 4 )] , where N ∼ Poisson (6) . This is because there are ( n 4 ) ways to pick the cards when there are n cards in the box. Thus, using LOTUS, E [( N 4 )] = ∞ ∑ n =0 ( n 4 ) · P [ X = n ] . However, we know that for n &lt; 4 , the binomial coefficient is 0 , so we can really start this summation at 4 , so this is ∞ ∑ n =4 n ! 4!( n -4)! · 6 n n ! e -6 = e -6 4! ∞ ∑ n =4 6 n ( n -4)! .

The trick here is to try to get a n -4 in the exponent of the 6 and then index shift our summation to be from n = 0 to ∞ . To do this, we take out 6 4 from the exponent to get 6 4 e -6 4! ∞ ∑ n =4 6 n -4 ( n -4)! . Now, we index shift back by 4 to let v = n -4 so that the sum ranges from v = 0 to ∞ and we obtain 6 4 e -6 4! ∞ ∑ v =0 6 v v ! . This last summation is just e 6 , so our answer is 6 4 = 54 .
