## Prompt:

A 6 -sided die shows the values 1 -6 with respective probabilities 1 / 6 , 1 / 5 -x, 1 / 10 + 2 x, 1 / 5 -x, 1 / 6 , and 1 / 6 . Find the value of x that maximizes the probability of obtaining a sum of 7 when you roll this die twice.

## Explanation:

You can obtain a sum of 7 by rolling a permutation of (1 , 6) , (2 , 5) , or (3 , 4) . The probability of a permutation of (1 , 6) is 2 · 1 / 6 · 1 / 6 = 1 / 18 . The probability of a permutation of (2 , 5) is 2 · 1 / 6 · (1 / 5 -x ) . The probability of a permutation of (3 , 4) is (1 / 10 + 2 x ) · (1 / 5 -x ) . Adding all of these and expanding, if p ( x ) represents the probability of obtaining a sum of 7 in 2 rolls with x as the input for our die,

$$p ( x ) = - 4 x ^ { 2 } + \frac { 4 } { 1 5 } x + \frac { 7 3 } { 4 5 0 }$$

We can use the formula x ∗ = -b 2 a to find the x -coordinate of the maximum. In particular, x ∗ = -4 / 15 -8 = 1 30 .
