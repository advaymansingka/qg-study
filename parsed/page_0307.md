## Prompt:

Let W t be a standard Brownian Motion. Compute Var (∫ t 0 W 2 s dW s ) . The answer will be in the form kt 3 for a constant k . Find k .

## Explanation:

As W 2 s is square-integrable, adapted, and continuous almost surely, ∫ t 0 W 2 s dW s is a martingale with mean 0 . By Ito Isometry, we have that

$$\mathbb { E } \left [ \left ( \int _ { 0 } ^ { t } W _ { s } ^ { 2 } d W _ { s } \right ) ^ { 2 } \right ] = \int _ { 0 } ^ { t } \mathbb { E } [ ( W _ { s } ^ { 2 } ) ^ { 2 } ] d s = \int _ { 0 } ^ { t } \mathbb { E } [ W _ { s } ^ { 4 } ] d s$$

As W s ∼ N (0 , s ) , W s = √ sZ , where Z ∼ N (0 , 1) . In particular, since E [ Z 4 ] = 3 , E [ W 4 s ] = E [( √ sZ ) 4 ] = 3 s 2 . Now, we evaluate evaluate that integral to simply be ∫ t 0 3 s 2 ds = t 3 . As we already know the integral is mean 0 , t 3 is also the variance, so k = 1 .

## 519 9 Appearance

## Prompt:

How many times does the digit 9 occur when counting 1 to 1000 ? Note that the number 919 , for example, would count as 2 9 s.

## Explanation:

The first thing is to count the number of times 9 appears in 1 -99 . Then, we can multiply this by 10 , as there are 10 starting digits 0 -9 that can go in front of this. A starting digit of 0 here really means that the number is less than 100 . Afterwards, we can add in 100 9 s afterwards for the 900 s to account for the starting digit of 9 .

9 occurs in each of 9 , 19 , . . . , 89 once, yielding 9 9 s. Afterwards, 90 -99 has 11 9 s, as 99 counts as 2 . Therefore, in 1 -99 , the digit 9 occurs 20 times. Thus, the answer is 10 · 20 + 100 = 300 .
